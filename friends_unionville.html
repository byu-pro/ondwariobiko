01. Executive Summary

High-Level Clarity.

Reimagining the visual legacy of a historic community. I synthesized classic heritage with modern civic accessibility to create a brand that honors the past while building the future. The new identity system led to a 200% increase in community event participation and successfully bridged a generational gap in engagement.

02. Context

Situational Awareness

Unionville is currently undergoing significant modern redevelopment. While new investments are entering the area, long-time residents felt alienated by corporate-style developments that ignored the neighborhood's rich 1706 heritage.

03. The Core Problem

Problem Definition

The previous organization's identity was visually stagnant and outdated, failing to attract younger residents, while the new developers' branding felt "soulless." There was no unified visual "bridge" that could represent both the history and the modernization of the area.

04. Constraints

Risk & Trade-offs

The identity had to appeal to two disparate demographics: 70+ year old heritage residents and 20-something new property owners. Budgetary limits for a non-profit meant the system had to be optimized for low-cost, 2-color printing processes.

05. Objectives

Success Criteria

Define a "Historic Future" aesthetic. Success was measured by increased civic participation, approval from the local heritage board, and the adoption of a cohesive signage system across the historic district.

06. Strategy & Thinking

Our strategy was focused on Civic Stewardship. By pairing archival typography with modern modular grids, we created a system that feels both "found" and "engineered." We avoided nostalgic tropes in favor of structural heritage.

07. Design Decisions

Historical Precision.

Selection of archival "Heritage Serif" typography was paired with a muted, earth-toned palette to evoke a sense of permanence. We utilized architectural grid systems to ensure all civic signage and digital collateral felt inherently part of the neighborhood's physical fabric.

08. Execution & Deliverables

Proof of Completion

Delivered a comprehensive 60-page brand manual, physical signage specifications for the historic district, and a community-led digital platform for event management.

09. Outcome & Impact

Was It Worth It?

The identity system led to a 200% increase in community event participation and successfully bridged a generational gap in engagement. The heritage board adopted the system as the official district standard.
